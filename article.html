I read my favourite book in this week...

In this week I was in England and I visited Brontë museum. It was a great opportunity that I buy this book in original version. Visiting the Brontë Parsonage Museum was a captivating journey into the lives and literary world of the Brontë sisters. The museum, located in Haworth, provides a glimpse into the modest parsonage where Charlotte, Emily, and Anne Brontë spent much of their lives. Upon entering the museum, the atmosphere felt steeped in history and creativity. Exhibits showcased personal belongings, manuscripts, and artifacts belonging to the Brontë family, offering a tangible connection to the authors and their works. The rooms were carefully preserved, allowing visitors to envision the daily lives of the Brontës and the settings that inspired their novels.

"Jane Eyre" is a classic novel written by Charlotte Brontë, published in 1847. The story follows the life of the protagonist, Jane Eyre, from her troubled childhood to her adult years. Orphaned at a young age, Jane is mistreated by her aunt and cousins and eventually sent to Lowood School, where she endures harsh conditions. As she grows older, Jane becomes a governess at Thornfield Hall, where she meets the enigmatic and brooding Mr. Rochester. A complex and compelling love story develops between Jane and Rochester, but secrets from Rochester's past threaten their happiness. The novel explores themes of social class, morality, and the struggle for independence, and it is celebrated for its strong, independent female protagonist who defies societal norms. "Jane Eyre" is considered a seminal work in the Victorian literary canon and continues to be widely read and studied for its exploration of human nature and social issues.
